## Gaps (1)

### G-1. Click-tracking beacon lifecycle after slot trim remains unspecified

**Spec section needing it**: §4.4.9, §8.3.

**Issue**: Carried forward from v5 G-3. v5.1 cannot close this in
a minor refinement: `context/` is silent on whether a viewer
click after slot trim (§4.4.3, E10) fires the click-tracking
beacon or suppresses it. Either reading is requirement-level.
Tracked in the Refinement gaps section of v5.1 (row 2).

**Implementer fallback today**: Same as v5 — Players either fire
opportunistically (per E12 fire-and-forget) or suppress after
trim (per the spirit of R13.3); both are defensible.

## Edge cases (1)

### EC-1. Hybrid slot where the overlay window is **longer** than the linear window

**Trigger**: Carried forward from v5 EC-1. Broadcaster authors a
hybrid (UC-04) where the linear `@maxDuration` is shorter than
the overlay `@maxDuration`.

**Why it matters**: §5.1.5 still encodes the clamp-to-linear-end
behaviour. The missing piece is the explicit UC-04 walkthrough
in `context/04-use-cases.md`. Context-level; tracked as
Refinement gaps row 3.

**Responsible actor**: Broadcaster declares; Player enforces
the clamp.

## Ambiguities (1)

### A-1. `<svta:Candidate>@priority` vs declared candidate order

**Spec passage**: Carried forward from v5 A-2. §5.2.2 says
`@priority` is informational; §4.4.4 says the Player honours
declared candidate order. The DP-1.2 single-source-of-truth
violation remains.

**Two readings**: (i) `@priority` is purely diagnostic; (ii)
`@priority` is a Player-side tie-break.

**Reading assumed by v5.1**: (i), unchanged from v5.

**Tighter context sentence**: `context/03-requirements.md` R7
SHOULD state whether `@priority` is purely diagnostic or a
Player-side tie-break. Tracked as Refinement gaps row 5.
