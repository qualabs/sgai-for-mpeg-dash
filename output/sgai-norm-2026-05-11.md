## Chapter 1 — Scope and Audience

### 1.1 Scope

This norm specifies Server-Guided Ad Insertion (SGAI) for both
**linear and non-linear ads** in MPEG-DASH, built as a complete
extension on top of MPEG-DASH 6th edition (ISO/IEC 23009-1:2025).

- **Linear SGAI** — the constructs introduced by MPEG-DASH 6th
  edition for linear advertising (`InsertPresentation`,
  `ReplacePresentation`, `ListMPD`) are absorbed as the baseline.
  Clarifications and minor extensions are added only where the
  gap analysis surfaces them.
- **Non-linear SGAI** — overlays, side-by-side compositions, pause
  ads, L-shapes, banners, fullscreen interactive layers, and the
  carrier for multi-form ad candidates that lets a Player select
  the highest-fidelity renderable form for its device class. This
  is the principal new content of this norm.

The norm covers the three-actor contract (Broadcaster, Ad Decision
Server, Player), the manifest extensions and resolution-document
shapes, the message flows and error semantics, and the conformance
criteria a runtime implementation MUST satisfy to claim compliance.

### 1.2 Out of scope

- Server-Side Ad Insertion / stitching (SSAI / SSR).
- Post-roll slots, companion ads, and native (in-chrome) ad
  integrations.
- The ADS's internal ad-decisioning logic (targeting, frequency
  capping, brand-safety filtering, ordering).
- A parallel layout standard for overlay placement — spatial
  arrangement is delegated to HTML5/CSS per R10.
- DRM, authentication, low-latency, ABR — orthogonal to SGAI.

### 1.3 Audience

- MPEG-DASH 6th edition implementers extending toward SGAI.
- SVTA Ads WG members developing companion implementation
  guidelines.
- ADS vendors integrating with DASH 6th players.
- Broadcasters authoring SGAI-enabled MPDs.

## Chapter 3 — Terms, Definitions, and Abbreviations

The full glossary lives in
[`../spec/99-glossary.md`](../spec/99-glossary.md). The terms
below are normative for this norm. Layout enum-values are
**canonical** — the norm MUST NOT invent new layout names at
chapter level (per R10).

### 3.1 Terms

- **SGAI (Server-Guided Ad Insertion)** — pattern where the ad
  decision is made server-side (by an ADS) rather than by the
  client; the MPD carries event references that the Player
  resolves into ad resolution documents.
- **Linear ad** — ad that *replaces* the primary content for the
  duration of a break.
- **Non-linear ad** — ad that *coexists* with the primary content
  during a slot (overlay or side-by-side composition).
- **Resolution document** — the document returned by the ADS in
  response to the Player's resolution request. In the linear
  baseline this is a `ListMPD` (§8.14). For non-linear slots this
  norm reuses the `ListMPD` carrier extended per chapter 5.
- **Renderable form** — one alternative way to render an ad
  candidate (video stream, image, HTML payload). A candidate
  carries one or more forms; the Player picks the highest-fidelity
  form it can render on the device (R5).
- **Layout token** — a value from the canonical layout vocabulary
  in `99-glossary.md` (banner, l-shape, side-by-side, etc.) that
  identifies the spatial role of an overlay without prescribing
  pixel coordinates.
- **Slot** — the timeline window (linear) or state-bound window
  (pause-triggered) in which one or more ads may render. Bounded
  by a Broadcaster-declared maximum duration (R4).
- **Pause-ad** — non-linear ad rendered only while primary content
  is in the *paused* state.

### 3.2 Abbreviations

- **ADS** — Ad Decision Server.
- **MPD** — Media Presentation Description.
- **SPS** — Single-Period Static Profile (§8.15).
- **CTV** — Connected TV.
- **CSS** — Cascading Style Sheets.
- **VAST** — Video Ad Serving Template (IAB Tech Lab).

### 3.3 Canonical layout vocabulary

Sourced from [`../spec/99-glossary.md`](../spec/99-glossary.md);
the table below pins the enum-values normative for this edition.
New layouts MUST be added to the glossary first, then referenced
here.

| Enum value     | Description                                                                 |
|----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `banner`       | Full-width strip across bottom (or top); primary keeps full scale.          |
| `l-shape`      | Primary shrunk to a corner; ad fills the remaining L along two edges.       |
| `skyscraper`   | Tall narrow vertical strip on one side.                                     |
| `sidebar`      | Vertical strip on one side, wider than skyscraper.                          |
| `side-by-side` | Primary scaled to one half; ad on the other half; no overlap.               |
| `squeezeback`  | Primary scaled inside a frame; ad UI surrounds it.                          |
| `pause-ad`     | Fullscreen overlay shown only while primary is paused.                      |
| `lower-third`  | Strip anchored to the lower third of the screen.                            |
| `corner-bug`   | Small element anchored to one corner.                                       |

## Chapter 4 — Conformance

### 4.1 Conformance levels

This norm defines three conformance roles aligned with the
three-actor model:

- **Broadcaster-conformant** — emits MPDs that declare SGAI slots
  per chapter 5 syntax, including the constraints required by R2
  / R4 on every slot.
- **ADS-conformant** — emits resolution documents per chapter 5
  syntax with the renderable-forms carrier, per R2 / R5.
- **Player-conformant** — implements the runtime obligations in
  chapter 7 (Expected behaviour) and chapter 9 (Error handling).

An implementation MAY claim conformance for one role or for
several. The Player role is the only one that imposes runtime
behaviour; the Broadcaster and ADS roles impose document-level
shape and field-level obligations.

### 4.2 RFC 2119 obligations summary

The following table lists the assertions a Player-conformant
implementation MUST satisfy at runtime. The corresponding ADS and
Broadcaster obligations are stated on the same row, where
relevant. The complete assertion table including assertions
inherited from the spec lives in
[`../analysis/conformance-assertions.md`](../analysis/conformance-assertions.md).

| ID | Actor | Obligation (summary) |
|----|-------|----------------------|
| R1.1 | Player | Ignore unknown SGAI constructs; continue primary playback. |
| R2.3 | Player | Validate every ADS candidate against MPD constraints; discard violators. |
| R3.2 | Player | Produce a defined behaviour (render / fall back / skip) for every supported (device, opportunity) pair. |
| R3.3 | Player | Never attempt to render a form the device cannot render. |
| R4.2 | Player | Stop at the Broadcaster-declared cap, even mid-ad. |
| R4.3 | Player | Never extend a slot beyond the cap. |
| R4.5 | Player | Enforce the cap against actual rendered length ("trim during play"). |
| R5.2 | Player | Among renderable forms, choose the best for the device. |
| R5.3 | Player | Skip candidates with no renderable form. |
| R6.5 | Player | Safely ignore unknown namespaces on tracking-related extensions. |
| R7.1 | Player | Honour ADS-declared candidate order (modulo R7.2, R7.3 drops). |
| R7.4 | Player | Never re-order, deduplicate, or rearrange remaining candidates. |
| R7.5 | Player | Trim mid-rendering when actual length exceeds the cap. |

### 4.4 Anti-patterns (reject during review)

- Placing a mandatory new element in a position whose legacy
  schema treats it as a required field with no extension hook.
- Reusing an existing element name from the baseline with new
  semantics.
- Introducing a construct whose semantics implicitly depend on a
  sibling that legacy Players cannot interpret, without a
  fallback path the legacy Player can execute.
- Using a scheme URI without a year suffix.

## Chapter 5 — Syntax

### 5.1 Namespaces and naming

New XML constructs introduced by this norm live in one of two
namespaces:

- **`urn:mpeg:dash:sgai:2026`** — proposed-MPEG additions
  (candidates for normative inclusion in a future DASH edition).
- **`urn:qualabs:sgai:2026`** — vendor experimental extensions.

Legacy Players MUST safely ignore unknown namespaces under DASH's
extension rules invoked by R1. The norm reuses the year-pinned
scheme URI convention established by MPEG-DASH 6th edition
(§5.16, §8.14): every new scheme URI introduced by this norm
ends in `:2026`.

### 5.2 `OverlayPresentation` event

#### 5.2.1 Element

A new XML element introduced under the `urn:mpeg:dash:sgai:2026`
namespace, carried as the child of an `<Event>` inside an
`<EventStream>` whose `@schemeIdUri` is
`urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-overlay:2026`.

```
<!ELEMENT OverlayPresentation EMPTY>
<!ATTLIST OverlayPresentation
  url                          CDATA          #REQUIRED
  earliestResolutionTimeOffset CDATA          #IMPLIED
  maxDuration                  CDATA          #REQUIRED
  allowedLayouts               CDATA          #REQUIRED  <!-- space-separated layout enum tokens -->
  maxConcurrentOverlays        CDATA          #IMPLIED
  executeOnce                  (true|false)   #IMPLIED
>
```

`<!-- TODO -->` — formal XSD lives in chapter 8 (Examples) as a
companion artefact; the DTD-style sketch above is illustrative.

#### 5.2.2 Attributes

- **`@url`** (REQUIRED) — URL the Player resolves against the
  ADS. Same semantics as `@url` on `InsertPresentation` (§5.16).
- **`@earliestResolutionTimeOffset`** (OPTIONAL) — same as the
  linear baseline. Default `0`.
- **`@maxDuration`** (REQUIRED) — Broadcaster-declared overlay
  duration cap. R4 enforcement applies verbatim:
  > "If the Alternative Presentation initiated by this event has
  > a longer duration than specified in this element, it shall be
  > terminated at the end of this duration" (§5.16.5).
- **`@allowedLayouts`** (REQUIRED) — space-separated list of
  layout tokens from the canonical vocabulary (chapter 3.3). The
  Player MUST reject candidates whose declared layout is outside
  this set.
- **`@maxConcurrentOverlays`** (OPTIONAL) — integer cap on the
  number of overlays the Broadcaster permits on screen
  simultaneously during this slot. Default `1`.
- **`@executeOnce`** (OPTIONAL) — same semantics as in §5.16.

#### 5.2.3 Legacy Player walk-through

A legacy Player encountering `<OverlayPresentation>`:

1. Parser sees an `<EventStream>` with an unrecognised
   `@schemeIdUri` (`urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-overlay:2026`).
2. Per §5.10.1, the Player subscribes to event streams of
   interest and ignores the rest. This event stream is ignored
   silently.
3. The remaining MPD parses unchanged because the unknown
   `<EventStream>` carries no required-sibling dependency on the
   `<Period>` or the `<AdaptationSet>`.
4. Playback of primary content continues uninterrupted.

#### 5.2.4 Required-sibling check

`<OverlayPresentation>` is optional in its parent (`<Event>`),
and its parent `<EventStream>` is optional under `<Period>`. No
legacy-required sibling is impacted by its presence or absence.
Removing the construct from a document leaves a valid DASH 6th
MPD.

### 5.3 Pause-trigger event

#### 5.3.1 Element

`<PauseTriggerPresentation>` under namespace
`urn:mpeg:dash:sgai:2026`, child of `<Event>` inside an
`<EventStream>` with `@schemeIdUri` =
`urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-pause-trigger:2026`.

#### 5.3.2 Attributes

- **`@url`** (REQUIRED) — ADS resolution URL.
- **`@maxDuration`** (REQUIRED) — pause-ad display cap.
- **`@allowedLayouts`** (REQUIRED) — typically restricted to
  `pause-ad` and/or fullscreen variants; values MUST come from
  the canonical vocabulary.

`@presentationTime` on the parent `<Event>` is a sentinel for
this scheme: the Player application logic decides *when* to
dispatch the ADS request based on local pause state. The
EventStream's value carries the action-trigger semantics; see
§7.5 (UC-05 behaviour).

Same shape as §5.2.3 (unknown EventStream → ignored per §5.10.1

### 5.4 `<RenderableForms>` extension on `<ImportedMPD>` / `<Period>`

#### 5.4.1 Element

A new XML child element introduced inside the `<Period>` records
of a `ListMPD` (§8.14). Lives in `urn:mpeg:dash:sgai:2026`.
Optional. When present, carries the candidate's alternative
renderable forms.

```
<sgai:RenderableForms>
  <sgai:Form type="video" mimeType="video/mp4" uri="creative_101_video.mpd" priority="1" duration="PT15S"/>
  <sgai:Form type="image" mimeType="image/png"  uri="creative_101.png"      priority="2" duration="PT15S"/>
  <sgai:Form type="html"  mimeType="text/html"  uri="creative_101.html"     priority="3" duration="PT15S"/>
</sgai:RenderableForms>
```

#### 5.4.2 `<sgai:Form>` attributes

- **`@type`** — `video` | `image` | `html` (extensible
  enum; new values MUST be declared in this norm or via vendor
  namespace).
- **`@mimeType`** — IANA media type.
- **`@uri`** — URL the Player fetches to render the form.
  `video` forms reference a sub-MPD (SPS, §8.15); `image` and
  `html` forms reference a media asset directly.
- **`@priority`** (OPTIONAL) — ADS-supplied ranking hint. Lower
  value = higher priority. The Player walks forms in priority
  order; ties broken by document order.
- **`@duration`** (OPTIONAL) — declared duration of the form. For
  `image` and `html` forms, drives R4 cap-trim against
  `@maxDuration`.

#### 5.4.3 Legacy Player walk-through

Per §5.2.1 of MPEG-DASH 6th edition (the MPD-level survival
rule): a DASH client that removes XML attributes or elements
that are not in the standard schema, or elements from other
namespaces, MUST still produce a valid XML document.
`<sgai:RenderableForms>` is in a non-DASH namespace
(`urn:mpeg:dash:sgai:2026`). Legacy Players strip the element
and process the `<ImportedMPD>` underneath unchanged. If the
legacy Player can render the SPS sub-MPD pointed to by
`<ImportedMPD>` it does so; if not, the candidate is silently
skipped (R3 fallback).

#### 5.4.4 Required-sibling check

`<sgai:RenderableForms>` augments the candidate; the
`<ImportedMPD>` sibling already carries the primary (video)
form. Removing `<sgai:RenderableForms>` leaves the per-Period
record valid as-is.

### 5.5 Vendor extension carrier for VAST application-layer metadata

Per R6.4, application-level VAST metadata that has no native
DASH carrier (`ClickThrough`, `AdSystem`, `AdTitle`,
`UniversalAdId`, ...) is conveyed via vendor-namespaced
extension elements on `<ImportedMPD>` or on a sidecar payload.
This norm RECOMMENDS the `urn:qualabs:sgai:2026` namespace as
the **default** carrier for non-finalised vendor metadata, with
the understanding that subsequent editions may promote the
specific field set to `urn:mpeg:dash:sgai:<year>` once SVTA Ads
WG and DASH WG converge.

Recommended attribute shape:

```
<qua:VastMetadata xmlns:qua="urn:qualabs:sgai:2026"
                  clickThrough="https://..."
                  universalAdId="..."
                  adSystem="..."
                  adTitle="..."/>
```

`<!-- TODO -->` — finalise field names and their multiplicity
(single vs list-of) per upstream VAST 4.x version pin.

### 5.6 Extension to ListMPD per-Period semantics

A `<Period>` in a ListMPD MAY now carry, in addition to
`<ImportedMPD>`:

- a `<sgai:RenderableForms>` element (per §5.4) holding the
  candidate's alternative forms,
- a `<qua:VastMetadata>` element (per §5.5) holding application-
  level VAST metadata,
- any vendor-namespaced metadata elements declared per chapter 6
  of [`../spec/06-naming-and-namespaces.md`](../spec/06-naming-and-namespaces.md).

All these elements are OPTIONAL. The `<ImportedMPD>` remains the
primary content carrier and is REQUIRED.

## Chapter 6 — Interfaces

This chapter pulls the linear Broadcaster ↔ ADS ↔ Player flow
from [`../spec/05-dash-linear-interfaces.md`](../spec/05-dash-linear-interfaces.md)
verbatim and extends it with the non-linear primitives.

### 6.1 Component inventory

| Actor       | Emits                                                                                | Consumes                                                                  |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Broadcaster | Main MPD with SGAI events (`InsertPresentation`, `ReplacePresentation`, `OverlayPresentation`, `PauseTriggerPresentation`) | Nothing at runtime (authoring-time only)                                  |
| Player      | MPD fetch request; ADS resolution request at event activation (or on pause for §5.3); tracking beacons | Main MPD; resolution document (`ListMPD`) from ADS; ad media segments     |
| ADS         | Resolution document (`ListMPD` extended per chapter 5)                                | Player's resolution request; upstream VAST response from internal ad decisioning |

### 6.2 Linear flow (baseline reuse)

Inherits unchanged from [`../spec/05-dash-linear-interfaces.md`](../spec/05-dash-linear-interfaces.md)
§ "Linear SGAI message flow". The 8-step flow (Player fetches
MPD → activates event near `presentationTime` → resolves ADS
URL → receives `ListMPD` → fetches ad segments → validates →
enforces cap → fires tracking) is normative as published in DASH
6th edition.

### 6.3 Non-linear overlay flow (new)

For an event of scheme
`urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-overlay:2026`:

1. Player issues `GET <event @url>` at a randomised instant
   between ERT and `presentationTime` (same trigger as linear).
2. ADS replies `200 OK` with an extended `ListMPD` body. Each
   `<Period>` MAY carry `<sgai:RenderableForms>` and
   `<qua:VastMetadata>`.
3. Player fetches the ad media (segments for `video` forms;
   direct URI for `image` / `html` forms).
4. Player **validates** each candidate against the
   Broadcaster-declared `@allowedLayouts` and
   `@maxConcurrentOverlays`. Candidates with no layout in the
   allowed set are discarded (R2).
5. For each surviving candidate, the Player walks
   `<sgai:RenderableForms>` in priority order and picks the
   first form whose `@type` the device can render (R5.2). If
   no form is renderable, the candidate is skipped (R5.3 / R7.2).
6. Player **enforces `@maxDuration`** on the overlay (R4). When
   the overlay's rendered display time reaches `@maxDuration`,
   the Player MUST remove the overlay, even mid-rendering.
7. Tracking is fired via the embedded callback `<EventStream>`
   inside the form's sub-MPD (for `video` forms) or via vendor
   sidecar declaration for `image` / `html` (`<!-- TODO -->`
   tracking-carrier syntax for non-video forms).

The primary content keeps playing throughout — the overlay flow
never pauses, jumps, or replaces the main timeline.

### 6.4 Pause-trigger flow (new)

For an event of scheme
`urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-pause-trigger:2026`:

1. Player observes the `<PauseTriggerPresentation>` event at MPD
   load time and records the slot's `@url` + `@maxDuration` +
   `@allowedLayouts` as state.
2. When the user pauses primary content, the Player issues
   `GET <event @url>`. **`[inferred]`**: a Player MAY pre-fetch
   the ADS response speculatively at MPD load; the norm does not
   mandate either policy. Trade-off: latency vs targeting
   freshness; documented in
   [`../analysis/dash-gap-analysis.md`](../analysis/dash-gap-analysis.md)
   Open question 4 of UC-03 / UC-05.
3. ADS responds with an extended `ListMPD`; processing proceeds
   per §6.3 steps 3–7 with the overlay composited on top of the
   paused primary frame.
4. When the user resumes playback, the Player MUST dismiss the
   overlay immediately, regardless of `@maxDuration` remaining.

### 6.5 ADS adapter (VAST → extended `ListMPD`)

Inherits unchanged from [`../spec/05-dash-linear-interfaces.md`](../spec/05-dash-linear-interfaces.md)
§ "VAST → ListMPD conversion" for linear ads. For non-linear:

- VAST `<NonLinearAds>` / `<NonLinear>` creatives map to one
  `<Period>` per non-linear creative in the extended `ListMPD`,
  each carrying `<sgai:RenderableForms>` populated from the
  VAST `<StaticResource>` / `<IFrameResource>` / `<HTMLResource>`
  triplet.
- VAST tracking events translate to callback events in the
  form's sub-MPD (for `video` forms) or — `<!-- TODO -->` —
  vendor-namespaced tracking entries for non-video forms.

| Player           | Tracking endpoints  | HTTP/HTTPS     | callback beacons (HTTP GET body-less)                           | fire-and-forget | Per `error-semantics.md` E10 (non-fatal). |
| ADS              | Upstream ad decisioning | HTTP/HTTPS | VAST 4.x request/response                                       | request/response| ADS-internal; surfaces to Player as E1..E4. |

All transport is over HTTPS in production. Authentication, DRM
and token exchange are layered on top of HTTPS per DASH-IF
guidance and are out of scope of this norm.

## Chapter 7 — Expected Behaviour Per Scenario

This chapter pulls the per-scenario, per-device-class expected
behaviour table from [`../spec/04-use-cases.md`](../spec/04-use-cases.md)
and binds it to the chapter-5 syntax.

### 7.1 UC-01 — Slot at start of session (pre-roll)

Broadcaster declares an `<InsertPresentation>` event with
`presentationTime="0"` and `@maxDuration` set. The flow follows
DASH 6th §5.16.2 baseline; no non-linear extension applies.

| Device class | Expected behaviour |
|--------------|--------------------|
| D1 | Picks highest-ranked renderable candidate; plays its video form on one decoder; enforces R4 at playback. |
| D2 | Same as D1; the second decoder and overlay surfaces are not exercised (linear-only). |
| D3 | Same flow on a single decoder (linear ads are sequential, not concurrent with primary). |
| D4 | Same as D3. |
| D5 | Same as D3. |

### 7.2 UC-02 — Mid-content slot (mid-roll)

Broadcaster declares a `<ReplacePresentation>` event at the slot
`presentationTime` with `@maxDuration`, `@returnOffset`,
`@clipDuration`, `@startWithOffset`. DASH 6th §5.16.3 baseline.

| Device class | Expected behaviour |
|--------------|--------------------|
| D1 | Same as UC-01 D1; second decoder MAY be used to pre-buffer the ad. |
| D2 | Same as D1; the second decoder MAY be used to pre-buffer. |
| D3 | Single decoder reused sequentially across primary → ad → primary. |
| D4 | Same as D3. |
| D5 | Same as D3. |

### 7.3 UC-03 — Coexisting overlay

Broadcaster declares an `<OverlayPresentation>` (§5.2) with
`@allowedLayouts` (e.g. `banner l-shape side-by-side corner-bug`),
`@maxConcurrentOverlays`, `@maxDuration`. ADS replies with
candidates carrying `<sgai:RenderableForms>`.

| Device class | Expected behaviour |
|--------------|--------------------|
| D1 | Validates candidates against allowed layouts; picks highest-priority form (typically video or HTML); composes via HTML/CSS layer (R10); enforces overlay cap at playback. |
| D2 | If candidate has a `video` form and a layout in `{side-by-side, banner via video-on-video composition}` is allowed, picks the video form. Else declines per R5.3 (HTML/image not composable on D2). |
| D3 | Skips `video` form (single decoder); picks `html` if available else `image`; composes on top via HTML/CSS layer. |
| D4 | Skips `html` and `video`; picks `image`; if no `image` form, the candidate is skipped (R5.3) and the next candidate is tried. |
| D5 | All forms non-renderable; per R5.3 the Player declines the opportunity entirely (primary continues uninterrupted). |

### 7.4 UC-04 — Hybrid linear + concurrent overlay

Broadcaster declares one `<ReplacePresentation>` for the linear
take-over portion and one `<OverlayPresentation>` covering the
same time span for the overlay portion. The two are independent
events.

| Device class | Expected behaviour |
|--------------|--------------------|
| D1 | Plays linear on one decoder; composes overlay on the second decoder (video form) or HTML/CSS layer (html/image form). |
| D2 | Linear on first decoder; overlay only if it has a `video` form (second decoder). If only `html`/`image`, overlay declined (R5.3) — linear plays alone, UX collapses to UC-02. |
| D3 | Linear plays alone (overlay declined; single-decoder + the "overlay on top of linear ad" composition is fragile on single-decoder devices). **`[inferred]`** — see UC-04 open question 2 in `04-use-cases.md`. |
| D4 | Same as D3. |
| D5 | Same as D3. |

### 7.5 UC-05 — Pause-triggered ad

Broadcaster declares a `<PauseTriggerPresentation>` (§5.3). The
Player tracks pause state and issues the ADS request on pause
(or pre-fetches per §6.4 implementation choice).

| Device class | Expected behaviour |
|--------------|--------------------|
| D1 | Walks renderable forms; picks highest-fidelity form; composes on top of paused frame; dismisses on resume. |
| D2 | `video` form rendered on second decoder over the paused frame; declines for `html`/`image`-only candidates (R5.3). |
| D3 | `html` form preferred; falls back to `image`; (`video` form on single decoder while paused is implementation-defined — `<!-- TODO -->` per Open question 2 in UC-05 / `04-use-cases.md`). |
| D4 | `image` form only. |
| D5 | Declines the slot entirely. |

### 7.6 UC-06 — Multi-ad break

Broadcaster declares one `<InsertPresentation>` / `<ReplacePresentation>`
event with `@maxDuration` representing the total break cap.
ADS returns a `ListMPD` whose `<Period>` entries together fill
the break.

The Player plays the periods in declared order (R7.1). R4 is
enforced against cumulative duration; the Player drops
candidates before play (R7.3) or trims mid-play (R7.5) when the
cap is approached or exceeded.

### 7.7 UC-07 — Legacy Player encounters new constructs

The Player encounters an `<EventStream>` with a `@schemeIdUri`
of `urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-overlay:2026` or
`urn:mpeg:dash:event:sgai-pause-trigger:2026` that it does not
implement. Per §5.10.1, the Player ignores the EventStream and
plays the primary content uninterrupted. No ad is rendered. No
tracking beacon is fired. No error is surfaced to the
application layer (unless the application explicitly opted in
to the surfacing API per §9.4).

Behaviour is uniform across D1..D5 because the graceful-skip
outcome depends on Player version, not on device hardware.

## Chapter 9 — Implementation Notes (non-normative)

This chapter pulls
[`../analysis/error-semantics.md`](../analysis/error-semantics.md)
into the norm as implementation guidance. The error matrix rows
(E1..E13) become the basis of the test cases in chapter 10.

### 9.1 Fall-through definition

"Fall through to primary content uninterrupted" means:

- The Player continues rendering the Broadcaster's primary stream
  without interrupting playback.
- No visible artefact of the failed SGAI exchange (no freeze, no
  blank slate, no error overlay unless the application explicitly
  opted in via §9.4).
- No tracking beacon fired for the failed exchange. Per R6,
  tracking is fired only against successfully rendered ads.

### 9.2 Error matrix (summary)

The full matrix lives in
[`../analysis/error-semantics.md`](../analysis/error-semantics.md);
the column structure is `ID | Error condition | Player response
(MUST) | Player response (MAY) | ADS / Broadcaster obligation`.
Rows of interest at a glance:

- **E1, E2, E3, E4, E12, E13** — transport, parse, schema,
  endpoint, late-response failures. Player MUST fall through to
  primary.
- **E5** — no candidate has a renderable form on the device.
  Player skips all and falls through.
- **E6** — drop-before-play when declared duration would push
  cumulative past the cap. Order preserved.
- **E7** — trim-during-play when actual length exceeds cap.
- **E8** — unknown layout token. Player treats the form as
  non-renderable.
- **E9** — unknown scheme URI or extension. R1 ignore-if-unknown.
- **E10** — tracking beacon HTTP failure. Non-fatal; never
  affects playback.
- **E11** — too-many-candidates. R7.3 drop-before-play applies
  in declared order.

### 9.3 Order of precedence (when multiple errors arise)

1. Transport / parse errors (E1, E2, E3, E12) — fall through
   immediately.
2. Resolution-document errors (E4, E13) — fall through.
3. Document-level constraint surfacing (E5, E11) — process
   per-candidate then fall through if the surviving set is empty.
4. Per-candidate violations (E6, E8, E9) — handled per candidate
   at decode time.
5. Playback-time violations (E7) — handled during rendering.
6. Tracking failures (E10) — non-fatal.

### 9.4 Surfacing errors to the application layer

The Player MAY expose error conditions via implementation-defined
APIs to the embedding application. This surface is **non-normative**.
Implementations are free to choose the API shape. What is
normative is the playback behaviour above.

### 9.5 Tracking-only VAST `<Ad>` entries

A `<Ad>` (Inline) entry from VAST that carries `<TrackingEvents>`
but no `<MediaFile>` cannot be synthesised into an ad sub-MPD with
media. The ADS adapter MAY silently omit such entries from the
`ListMPD`. This norm does not mandate a single policy — silent
omission and VAST Error code 403 emission are both acceptable;
the choice is ADS-internal. `<!-- TODO -->` — pin the policy in
the next iteration if WG feedback consolidates.

### 9.6 Late ADS responses

Per E13, if a resolution document arrives after the slot window
has elapsed, the Player MUST discard it and continue with primary
content uninterrupted. The Player MUST NOT play late ads outside
their slot. For overlay slots specifically, "the slot window has
elapsed" means the overlay's `@maxDuration` time after the
event's `presentationTime` has passed.

### 9.7 Pre-fetching pause-ad resolution

§6.4 step 2 leaves pre-fetching of pause-ad ADS responses as an
implementation choice. Recommendation: a Player SHOULD pre-fetch
at MPD load if the response is expected to be cacheable (no
short TTL on the ADS response) and the latency cost on pause is
otherwise unacceptable. Otherwise defer to pause-time fetch for
targeting freshness.

### 9.8 ADS request on devices that decline all candidates

For devices that cannot render any form on offer (D5 in most
non-linear scenarios), whether the Player issues the ADS request
purely for impression accounting and frequency-capping signals is
a Player implementation choice. The norm does not mandate either.
A privacy- and bandwidth-conscious implementation MAY skip the
request; an ad-business-aligned implementation MAY issue it and
treat the resolution document as decision-without-render telemetry.
**`[inferred]`** — pending WG / SVTA Ads WG guidance.

### 10.1 UC-07 reference harness

Per [`../spec/07-backward-compat-checklist.md`](../spec/07-backward-compat-checklist.md)

- Use a Player implementation predating the construct under
  test (a DASH 6th edition-only Player).
- Feed it an MPD/ListMPD containing the construct.
- Pass criteria: observable playback of primary content
  continues uninterrupted; no FATAL-level errors; no tracking
  beacon for the unknown construct.
